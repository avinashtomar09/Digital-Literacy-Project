CYBERCRIME AWARENESS CASE STUDY
Topic: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

WHAT IS UPI FRAUD?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where
scammers trick victims into revealing their UPI PIN, OTP, or account
details in order to steal money directly from their bank accounts.

HOW IT TYPICALLY HAPPENS (Step-by-Step):
1. The victim receives a call or WhatsApp message from someone
   posing as a bank official, customer care agent, or even a friend.
2. The scammer creates urgency, claiming the victim's account will
   be blocked, or that they have received a payment and need to
   "collect" it.
3. The victim is sent a UPI collect request or a fake link that
   looks like an official bank page.
4. The victim enters their UPI PIN thinking they are receiving money
   but they are actually approving a payment out of their account.
5. Money is instantly debited and transferred to the scammer's account.
6. The scammer disconnects and becomes unreachable.

WHO IS USUALLY TARGETED?
First-time UPI users and college students
Senior citizens unfamiliar with digital payments
Online shoppers on platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace
Anyone expecting a refund or payment

CONSEQUENCES:
Immediate financial loss (often thousands of rupees)
No guaranteed recovery as UPI transactions are instant
Emotional distress and loss of trust in digital payments
In some cases, victims take loans to recover losses
